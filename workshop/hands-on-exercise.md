# Hands-On Exercise — "Catch the Mistake" (15 minutes)

The goal of this activity is to build the single most important habit: **always check the
plain-language version against the original.** AI tools are useful drafters but they can
miss or garble a detail, and in benefits work a missing deadline matters.

## What you'll have
- A printed **original notice** (from `sample_docs/`).
- Its **plain-language version** produced by the tool.
- This checklist.

## Your task
Read the plain-language version, then compare it to the original and answer:

1. **Deadlines** — Is every date from the original present and correct in the rewrite?
   List any that are missing or changed.
2. **Money** — Is every dollar amount correct? (Watch for rounded or invented numbers.)
3. **Actions** — Does the rewrite clearly say what the reader must DO and BY WHEN?
4. **No extras** — Does the rewrite state anything that is NOT in the original? Circle it.
5. **Escalation** — Does this notice describe a termination, denial, overpayment, or
   appeal? If so, did the tool flag it for a human (`escalate: true`)? Should it have?

## Discussion (bring back to the group)
- What did you catch, if anything?
- Was the rewrite easier to understand than the original? Where could it be clearer?
- For this notice, would you send the tool's version as-is, edit it, or hand it to a
  caseworker? Why?

## Takeaway
> The tool drafts. **You decide.** Two minutes checking deadlines and amounts against the
> original is the difference between a helpful tool and a harmful one.
